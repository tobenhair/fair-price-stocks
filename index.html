Company by company

Moat · products · checklist

The moat gauge shows defensive width on a five-ring scale — the more rings filled, the harder the castle is to attack. The six-box checklist marks Buffett's classic tests: understandable business, consistent earnings, ROE ≥ 15%, conservative debt, durable moat, growing earnings.

How the numbers are made

Quality score (0–100)

Return on equity (25 p), return on invested capital (15 p), net margin (10 p), ten-year earnings consistency (20 p), moat rating (25 p) and balance-sheet conservatism (5 p). Banks and investment companies are scored without ROIC/debt where those metrics don't apply.

Margin of safety

(Fair value − price) ÷ fair value. Fair values are simple, conservative estimates — owner-earnings DCF for operating companies, NAV discount for investment companies, cyclically-adjusted multiples for TRATON and Ericsson. They are anchors for thinking, not predictions.

Circle of competence

Pre-profit companies (Acconeer, Midsummer) and opaque venture portfolios (Flat Capital) fail Buffett's first filter regardless of technology or story: no consistent earnings means no calculable intrinsic value, and therefore no margin of safety.
